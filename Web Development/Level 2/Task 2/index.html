Nikola Tesla

The visionary inventor who helped power the modern world.

Biography

Nikola Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist. He is best known for his contributions to the development of modern alternating current (AC) electricity systems.

Born in 1856, Tesla displayed an extraordinary talent for science and engineering from an early age. Throughout his career, he worked on numerous groundbreaking inventions and patents that transformed the way electricity is generated and distributed.

Tesla's innovations laid the foundation for many technologies used today, including wireless communication, electric motors, and radio systems. His ideas often extended far beyond the technology of his time, making him one of history's most visionary thinkers.

Although he faced financial difficulties and received less recognition than some of his contemporaries during his lifetime, Tesla's legacy continues to inspire scientists, engineers, and innovators around the world.

Major Achievements

1884

Moved to the United States and began working with Thomas Edison.

1888

Developed the AC induction motor and promoted AC power systems.

1893

Demonstrated AC electricity at the Chicago World's Fair.

1895

Helped establish the Niagara Falls hydroelectric power project.

1898

Demonstrated one of the world's first radio-controlled devices.
